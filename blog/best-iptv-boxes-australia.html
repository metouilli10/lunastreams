What is an IPTV box?

An IPTV box is a dedicated streaming device that connects to your TV via HDMI and delivers IPTV content over your internet connection. Unlike Smart TVs with built-in apps, dedicated IPTV boxes offer more processing power, better codec support, and often include features like hardware-accelerated video decoding for smoother playback.

IPTV boxes come in two main categories:

  • Android TV boxes: Run Android TV OS, giving you access to Google Play Store apps plus IPTV players like TiviMate, Perfect Player, and XCIPTV. These are versatile devices that work with Luna Streams and other streaming services.
  • MAG boxes: Purpose-built IPTV devices running proprietary firmware optimised for IPTV streaming. They offer a cable-like experience with EPG integration but require manual portal configuration.

Many Australian households also use streaming sticks (like Amazon Fire TV Stick) for IPTV, which we cover in our Firestick installation guide. However, dedicated boxes typically offer better performance and more storage for recording catch-up content.

Key features to consider

When choosing an IPTV box for Australian use, consider these factors:

1. Processing power and RAM

More powerful processors handle high-bitrate streams and multiple apps better. For smooth 4K IPTV playback, look for at least a quad-core processor and 2GB RAM. The Nvidia Shield TV Pro leads here with its Tegra X1+ chip and 3GB RAM.

2. Connectivity options

Ethernet ports provide more stable streaming than Wi-Fi, especially important for NBN connections. If your box only has Wi-Fi, ensure it supports 5GHz bands for better performance. Many Australian households benefit from wired connections for IPTV to avoid buffering.

3. Storage capacity

Storage matters if you plan to record catch-up content or install multiple apps. Android TV boxes typically offer 8–16GB, while Apple TV provides 64GB or 128GB options. Some devices support USB expansion for additional storage.

4. Codec support

Ensure your box supports modern video codecs (H.264, H.265/HEVC) and audio formats (AAC, AC3, EAC3). Most modern boxes handle these, but older budget models may struggle with HEVC streams common in IPTV services.

5. Operating system compatibility

Android TV boxes offer the widest app selection, including TiviMate, Perfect Player, and XCIPTV. MAG boxes use proprietary firmware but provide a simpler, cable-like interface. Choose based on your technical comfort level and desired features.

6. Price and availability in Australia

Consider local warranty support and availability. Devices sold through Australian retailers (JB Hi-Fi, Harvey Norman, Amazon Australia) typically include local warranties, while imported boxes may require international shipping for repairs.

MAG boxes

MAG (Multimedia Application Gateway) boxes are purpose-built IPTV devices from Infomir. They run Stalker Middleware, a proprietary IPTV platform optimised for streaming live TV.

MAG box models available in Australia

  • MAG 322: Entry-level model with HD support, perfect for budget-conscious viewers
  • MAG 324: Mid-range option with improved processing power
  • MAG 425A: Premium model with 4K support and advanced features

Pros and cons of MAG boxes

Advantages:

  • Dedicated IPTV interface (no app installation needed)
  • Built-in EPG support
  • Time-shift and recording capabilities
  • Stable performance with minimal buffering
  • Compatible with Luna Streams portal

Disadvantages:

  • Limited to IPTV use (can't install other apps)
  • Requires manual portal configuration
  • Less flexible than Android TV boxes
  • Older models don't support 4K
