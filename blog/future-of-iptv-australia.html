Growth of Streaming in Australia

Streaming has become the dominant entertainment format in Australian households. Recent data shows that over 85% of Australian homes now subscribe to at least one streaming service, with IPTV subscriptions growing faster than traditional cable and satellite combined. This shift accelerated during the NBN rollout, which brought reliable high-speed internet to regional and metropolitan areas alike.

Statistics

According to industry reports, IPTV adoption in Australia has increased by over 200% since 2020. By 2025, analysts predict that IPTV will account for more than 40% of all paid TV subscriptions in Australia, up from just 15% in 2020. The average Australian household now streams over 25 hours of content per week, with live sports and international channels driving much of this growth.

Key statistics shaping the future:

  • Over 12 million Australian households have access to NBN speeds sufficient for HD IPTV streaming
  • IPTV subscriptions are growing at 35% year-over-year, compared to 5% for traditional pay TV
  • Multi-device streaming (watching on 3+ devices simultaneously) has increased by 180% since 2022
  • Regional Australia now accounts for 30% of new IPTV subscriptions, up from 12% in 2020

NBN adoption

The National Broadband Network (NBN) rollout has been the foundation for IPTV growth in Australia. With over 8.5 million active NBN connections and fibre-to-the-premises (FTTP) expanding to more suburbs, Australian households now have the infrastructure needed for seamless IPTV streaming. The shift from ADSL to NBN has enabled consistent HD and 4K streaming that was previously impossible in many areas.

As NBN continues to upgrade existing connections to faster tiers and expand fibre coverage, IPTV providers like Luna Streams can deliver higher-quality streams with lower latency. This infrastructure growth directly correlates with increased IPTV adoption, especially in areas where traditional cable TV was never available. Why IPTV Is Becoming Mainstream

Several factors are driving IPTV from a niche alternative to mainstream entertainment choice in Australia. Cost savings, content diversity, and the shift toward on-demand viewing have made IPTV the preferred option for millions of Australian households.

Cost

Traditional cable packages like Foxtel can cost $100–$150 per month once sports and premium channels are included. IPTV plans start under $30 per month, offering comparable or superior channel lineups at a fraction of the cost. This price advantage is particularly appealing to families and budget-conscious viewers who want premium content without premium pricing. The elimination of installation fees, equipment rentals, and long-term contracts further reduces the total cost of ownership.

As IPTV providers scale their infrastructure and optimise content delivery, prices are expected to remain competitive while quality improves. Content

IPTV providers offer access to thousands of channels from around the world, including live sports, international news, movies, and entertainment that aren't available on traditional Australian cable. Unlike cable packages that lock you into predefined channel groups, IPTV allows viewers to customise their channel lists and access content from multiple countries in one subscription.

This content diversity is especially valuable for Australia's multicultural population, who can access channels from their home countries alongside Australian free-to-air and premium content. IPTV also includes extensive video-on-demand (VOD) libraries, combining live TV with on-demand movies and series in a single platform.

On-demand culture

Australian viewers increasingly prefer watching content on their own schedule rather than adhering to broadcast timetables. IPTV platforms integrate live TV with comprehensive catch-up and on-demand libraries, allowing viewers to watch what they want, when they want, on any device. This flexibility aligns with modern viewing habits, where binge-watching and time-shifted viewing are the norm.

The on-demand culture extends to sports as well, with IPTV providers offering match replays, highlights, and multi-angle coverage that traditional broadcasters can't match. This shift toward viewer-controlled content consumption is a fundamental reason why IPTV is becoming mainstream in Australia.

The Rise of NBN, 5G & Fibre

Australia's internet infrastructure is evolving rapidly, with NBN upgrades, 5G mobile networks, and fibre expansion creating new possibilities for IPTV streaming. These technological advances are removing the barriers that previously limited IPTV adoption in regional and metropolitan areas.

The NBN continues to upgrade connections from fixed wireless and fibre-to-the-node (FTTN) to fibre-to-the-premises (FTTP), delivering speeds of 100 Mbps to 1 Gbps to more Australian homes. These speeds enable seamless 4K streaming, multi-device households, and low-latency live sports viewing that wasn't possible on older ADSL connections.

5G mobile networks are also transforming IPTV, allowing viewers to stream high-quality content on mobile devices without relying on Wi-Fi. As 5G coverage expands across Australia, mobile IPTV viewing is becoming increasingly viable, especially for commuters and viewers who want entertainment on the go.

Fibre-to-the-premises (FTTP) connections offer the best IPTV experience, with symmetrical upload and download speeds that eliminate buffering and support multiple simultaneous streams. As NBN continues its fibre expansion program, more Australian households will have access to these premium connections, further accelerating IPTV adoption.

These infrastructure improvements mean IPTV providers can deliver higher-quality streams, support more concurrent users, and offer advanced features like cloud DVR and multi-screen viewing. Smart Homes & Multi-Device Streaming

Smart home integration and multi-device streaming are defining the future of IPTV in Australia. Modern Australian households expect to watch content on any device, at any time, with seamless transitions between screens. IPTV platforms are evolving to meet these expectations.

Smart TVs are now the primary IPTV viewing device in Australian homes, with Samsung Tizen, LG webOS, and Android TV supporting native IPTV apps. Voice assistants like Google Assistant and Amazon Alexa are being integrated into IPTV platforms, allowing viewers to control playback, search for content, and switch channels using voice commands.

Multi-device streaming is becoming standard, with IPTV providers offering plans that support simultaneous streaming on multiple devices. A typical Australian household might watch IPTV on the living room Smart TV, a bedroom tablet, and a mobile phone simultaneously—all from a single subscription.

Key smart home and multi-device trends:

  • Unified experience: Start watching on one device and continue on another without interruption
  • Parental controls: Manage content access across all devices from a single dashboard
  • Home automation integration: Control IPTV playback through smart home systems
  • Personalised profiles: Each family member gets customised channel lists and viewing preferences
